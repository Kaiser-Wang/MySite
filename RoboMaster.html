RoboMaster Robotics Competition

RoboMaster is an annual robotics competition for teams of aspiring engineers to design and build next-generation robots for completing difficult tasks and hand-to-hand combat. I joined 2018 RoboMaster student club in University of Alberta. In a mechanical engineering team, we designed and built a standard infantry/soldier robot capable of moving omni-directionally and launching 17 mm projectiles.

Description of Project

In this project, our team accomplished the following tasks:

  • Identified competition rules and design specificiations.
  • Investigated the previous infantry robot designed by other students and existing infantry robot designed by DJI.
  • Defined previous problems and proposed the solutions and improvements.

  • Identified available components, resources and materials for prototyping and manufacturing.
  • Collaborated with electrical team and AI team for the design of the robot.
  • Designed new non-independent suspension system, shooting mechanism and pan-tilt platform in SolidWorks.
  • Designed robot structures and frames, and mounts for motors, cameras, lidars, bumpers, armors, electrical modules, wheel adapters and so on.
  • Built the robot with T slots, aluminum alloy plate, CNC parts, 3D printed parts, etc.
  • Studied the kinematics of four Mecanum wheeled robots. (Reference: Mecanum wheels - Ecam Eurobot)

  • Tested the teleoperation of the robot for moving omni-directionally.

  • Tested the teleoperation of the robot for shooting 17 mm projectiles.

    • Result

    A standard infantry/soldier robot was designed to have a non-independent suspension system with four Mecanum wheels, a pan-tilt gimbal platform, and a shooting mechanism. The robot was able to move omni-directionally, and aim and launch in pitch and yaw angles. However, there were still some problems such as the jamming of projectiles, the bending of motor shafts and the unstability of suspension system. The RoboMaster club participated in ICRA 2018 and RoboMaster Robotics Competition 2018 on behalf of University of Alberta.
