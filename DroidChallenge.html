The Star wars Droid Challenge

This was my first mechanical design project. The objective of “The Star Wars Droid Challenge” was to design and build a droid capable of navigating a multi-terrain course using a remote control and spotting the targets with scores placed throughout the course with an on-board camera.

Description of Project

In this project, our team accomplished the following tasks:

  • Made a Gantt chart for the project and a decision matrix for different designs.

  • Designed a three-wheeled differential-drive droid in SolidWorks. The pivot wheel can be fixed as a skid steer.
  • Tested, plotted and analyzed the torque vs speed curve of motors.
  • Determined the droid's weight, speed, torque, gear ratio, friction, slipping, tipping, and overall performance.
  • Calculated the camera's field of view.
  • Made drawings and BOM for each part and assembly in SolidWorks.
  • Calculated tolerance for bearings, holes and shafts.
  • Fabricated sheet steel using water-jet cut and bending machine.
  • Manufactuerd bearing blocks with polyethylene by cutting and drilling.
  • Manufactured acrylic gears by laser-cut and polycarbonate gears by water-jet cut.
  • 3D printed tire with rubber and rim with ABS.

    • Result

    On the test day, the robot was able to go through the three courses and scan the targets with the camera. However, due to the time limit and lack of practice, the robot was not able to finish scanning all the targets and enter the finish zone within the alloted time. The ranking on the test day was something the group could be proud of, although not as well as our lofty goals. Overall, the group felt that the final design performed well.
