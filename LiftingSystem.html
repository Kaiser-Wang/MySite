Grappling Hook Lifting System

This design project was undertaken by a team of six mechanical engineering students. The objective of the project was to design a small and light grappling hook lifting system capable of lifting a 90 kg person to 15 meters high with an average speed of 2 m/s.

Description of Project

In this project, our team accomplished the following tasks:

  • Defined design specifications through conversation with customer and made a Gantt chart for the project.
  • Prepared seven design concepts by creative brainstorming and four design concepts were approved by all group members.
  • Selected preliminary design through consultation with customer and a decision matrix with six criteria: safety, stability, weight, cost, complexity, and maneuverability.
  • Prepared detailed design calculations and analysis of power, transmission, gear, shaft and bearing.
  • Determined the overall safety factor or the nominal safety factor to guide the calculations for the design.
  • Selected motor and battery based on the power, torque and speed analysis.
  • Designed a planetary gear set consisting of a sun gear, four planetary pinions, and a ring gear. (Planetary Gear Simulator)

  • Calculated gear reduction, gear ratio and contact ratio between gears from transmission analysis, and selected gears based on the bending stress analysis.
  • Selected gears based on the hardness and bending fatigue strength of material. Calculated safety factor of bending strength and safety factor of contact stress.
  • Considered shaft material properties such as yield strength, ultimate strength, brittleness, and mass density. Calculated material index for minimum mass design and fracture stress at maximum moment of shaft, and finally selected AISI 1080 Carbon Steel (UNS G10800) as shaft material from Ashby chart.

  • Calculated the shaft diameter based on the fatigue, angle of deflection and shear stress.
  • Selected different bearings based on the calculation of load and life at certain reliability.
  • Sized key and keyway between shaft and plate, dowel pins between gear and plate, and bolts in shell.
  • Determined the connection between ring gear and shell, and general casing and structure. Selected rope, hook, spear gun, guiding pulley and lubricating oil.
  • Designed a 3D model in SolidWorks and prepared assembly and parts drawings suitable for manufacture.

    • Conclusion

    The design challenge of the grappling hook lifting system is to lift a 90kg person with an average speed of 2m/s. In order to meet this target, four design concepts were generated from the brainstorming session. After consideration of safety, stability, complexity, maneuverability, weight and cost, the preliminary design was selected and analyzed in aspects of power, transimission, gear, bearing, shaft, material and so on. The hook lifting system - AGC I, has weight of 21.5 kg. The shaft and dowel pin material is AISI 1080 Carbon Steel. The gear material is Carburized and case hardened steel. The shell is made of 3004-O, Rod(SS), and bolts are made of steel. All calculated safety factors are greater than nominal safety factor (3.3). The detailed design primarily focused on the safety of the product. A prototype should be made to test practical performance and gather technical data so the product can be further improved. Overall, the final design was expected to meet the requirements of the bid proposal.
